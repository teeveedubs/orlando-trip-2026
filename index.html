Revenge of the Mummy
~10:00–10:25 AM
Intense indoor roller coaster — dark, launches forward and backward. Height: 48". NO Express Pass.
No Express Pass48" — both kids clear
▾
Ride type: Indoor steel roller coaster with a launch mechanism and reverse section. Entirely indoors in complete or near-complete darkness for most of the ride.
The story: You're on a film set for a Mummy movie and get pulled into the actual curse. The vehicle launches forward into the darkness, then a fake ending tricks you before a backward launch sends you flying in reverse. Mummies, scarabs, fireballs, and Imhotep appear throughout.
Physical sensations: Very intense. Darkness makes every turn and drop feel faster and more disorienting. The backward launch is the most startling moment. Fire effects (real heat) and ceiling elements that drop toward you. Top speed ~45 mph.

No Express: Go early — cannot be bypassed with Express later. Waits hit 60–90 min by midday.
Family verdict: Adults and 10 yr old — absolutely ride this. For the 7.5 yr old, discuss what to expect: dark, fast, jump scares. If they're game they'll probably love it. Child Swap available if needed.

Hogwarts Express — King's Cross to Hogsmeade
~5:40–6:10 PM
Board at King's Cross in Diagon Alley. Ride one-way to Hogsmeade, exit IOA through CityWalk to The Kitchen. Or round trip (~30 min) and arrive ~15 min late. No additional tickets needed.
Harry PotterPark-to-Park ticket required — you have it
▾
What it is: A fully immersive train journey between King's Cross Station in Diagon Alley (USF) and Hogsmeade Station (IOA). Each direction tells a completely different story through the compartment windows, door, and overhead luggage rack, using projection, audio, practical effects, and surprising physical moments.
USF → IOA (King's Cross to Hogsmeade): Harry, Ron, and Hermione are hiding from Death Eaters and Snatchers on the train. Silhouettes of threats pass by outside your compartment door. Effects include a genuinely startling Dementor moment. The arrival into Hogsmeade — seeing Hogwarts Castle through the window as the train slows — is one of the most magical moments in any theme park.
IOA → USF (Hogsmeade to King's Cross): The Weasley twins try to sell you Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes products, magical creatures escape from luggage (including a memorable Cornish Pixie moment), and the tone is funny rather than tense. This direction plays on Day 3 if you ride from Hogsmeade.
Route optimized: San Francisco is adjacent to Diagon Alley — after Fast & Furious, walk directly into Diagon Alley and board at King's Cross. The optimized route brings you back to this land naturally with zero backtracking.
Dinner logistics: One-way ride to IOA takes ~15 min total. Exit IOA, walk through CityWalk to Hard Rock Hotel (~10 min). Arrive ~6:05–6:10. If you want both directions, do the round trip and arrive ~6:15–6:20 — The Kitchen will hold your table.
Tickets: Your 3-Day Park-to-Park tickets cover Hogwarts Express. Express Pass does not apply — but there's no meaningful standby wait. The train departs every few minutes.
Day-of check: Confirm in the Universal app that the Hogwarts Express is running. It occasionally goes down for technical reasons. If it's down on Day 1, the Day 3 tentative option becomes your backup.
Family verdict: One of the most unique experiences in any theme park — it's not a ride, it's a story. The USF → IOA direction with the Dementor moment and Hogwarts Castle reveal is genuinely breathtaking. Do not skip this.

When to do this: After Caro-Seuss-el at ~4:45 PM, walk through Lost Continent to Hogsmeade Station (~10 min). If you already did both directions on Day 1, skip this and walk straight to CityWalk from the IOA exit — you'll arrive at Toothsome by 5:00 PM with plenty of time to spare.
IOA → USF (Hogsmeade to King's Cross): The Weasley twins try to sell you Wizard Wheezes products, magical creatures escape from luggage, and the tone is funny and warm. A lovely contrast to the intensity of the rest of Day 3.
USF → IOA (King's Cross to Hogsmeade): If you ride the return leg, this direction features the Dementor moment and Hogwarts Castle reveal. The more emotionally powerful of the two.
Dinner logistics: One-way ride (IOA → USF) deposits you at King's Cross Station inside USF. Exit USF, walk through CityWalk to Toothsome (~10 min). Arrive ~5:25–5:35 PM — comfortably ahead of your 6:00 PM reservation.
Or skip the Express: From Seuss Landing, walk directly to the IOA exit through Port of Entry → CityWalk → Toothsome in ~15 minutes. Arrive ~5:00 PM with an hour to spare. Use the extra time to browse CityWalk shops.
Tickets: Your 3-Day Park-to-Park tickets cover Hogwarts Express. No additional purchase needed.
Day-of check: Confirm in the Universal app that the Hogwarts Express is running before planning around it.
Family verdict: If you skipped it on Day 1, absolutely make time for it here. If you already did it, skip and enjoy a relaxed walk to dinner. A beautiful way to close out the Harry Potter chapter of the trip either way.
🍽 Dinner · Toothsome Chocolate Emporium · 6 PM
🍴
Toothsome Chocolate Emporium & Savory Feast Kitchen
~6:00 PM
Steampunk chocolate factory restaurant in CityWalk with spectacular milkshakes and character encounters.
▾
What it is: Set in a steampunk-themed Victorian chocolate factory. The exterior — a towering steampunk factory with pipes, gears, and venting steam — is worth photographing before you go in.
The savory menu: Substantial and genuinely good — pasta, burgers, steaks, salmon, flatbreads. Don't fill up too fast — save room for dessert.
The desserts: The main event. Toothsome's milkshakes are enormous, over-the-top creations topped with brownies, cake slices, candy, and whipped cream. The chocolate fondue and specialty dessert plates are genuinely excellent.
The characters: Toothsome and Penelope, the steampunk proprietors, walk the restaurant floor in full costume for photo opportunities throughout the meal.
Location & logistics: Universal CityWalk — exit IOA through the main gate, walk through CityWalk (~5 minutes). From Seuss Landing give yourself 15 minutes.
Family verdict: The perfect final dinner of the trip. The milkshakes alone will be a memory both kids talk about for years. End the trip here.
